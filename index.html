C:\> SLIDE 1 // WHAT_ARE_TRANSISTORS.EXE

A transistor is a tiny semiconductor device that can amplify or switch electronic signals and power. It is the fundamental building block of all modern electronics.


Made from semiconductor materials — most commonly silicon — the transistor replaced the bulky, power-hungry vacuum tube in the 1950s and set off a revolution in electronics.

  • Controls large currents with small voltages
  • Acts as an amplifier OR a digital switch
  • Three terminals: Base · Collector · Emitter
  • Made from N-type & P-type semiconductor layers
  • Can switch ON/OFF billions of times per second
  • BJT — Bipolar Junction Transistor (NPN / PNP)
  • MOSFET — Metal-Oxide Semiconductor FET
  • JFET — Junction Field-Effect Transistor

  • John Bardeen

  • William Shockley

They received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1956.

C:\> SLIDE 2 // HOW_DO_THEY_WORK.EXE

Think of a transistor as a light switch — but instead of your finger flipping it, a small electrical signal at the Base terminal controls whether current flows between Collector and Emitter.


OFF (0): No base signal → no current flows → circuit is open.

ON (1): Small base signal → large current flows → circuit is closed.


This binary behavior — 0 and 1 — is the foundation of all digital computing. Every bit in your computer is stored & processed by transistors switching on and off.

  • Silicon is doped with impurities to create P and N regions
  • N-type: extra electrons (negative charge carriers)
  • P-type: electron holes (positive charge carriers)
  • Junctions between P/N layers create a depletion zone
  • Base voltage modulates the depletion zone to allow/block flow
TRANSISTOR: ON
Base signal present → current flows
Bit value = 1

In analog mode, a transistor doesn't just switch — it proportionally amplifies. A tiny signal at the Base produces a much larger, identical waveform at the output. This powers radios, audio amps, and sensors.

C:\> SLIDE 5 // SOCIETY_TODAY.EXE

Modern AI chips (GPUs, TPUs) pack billions of transistors to perform the matrix math powering large language models, image recognition, and self-driving cars.


NVIDIA's H100 GPU: 80 billion transistors. AI would be impossible without them.

The phone in your pocket contains a chip with 15–20 billion transistors, more computing power than all of NASA in 1969.


5G networks — enabled by advanced transistor design — connect 8 billion people and enable real-time global communication.

Power transistors control solar inverters, EV motors, and smart grids. They are essential to the renewable energy transition.


But: data centers running on transistors now consume ~2% of global electricity — a growing environmental concern.

  • Transistors are now 2–3 nanometers wide — just 10 atoms across
  • Moore's Law is slowing — physical limits of silicon are approaching
  • 3D stacking (chiplets) is extending performance gains
  • Quantum computing may eventually surpass transistor-based logic
  • Carbon nanotube and graphene transistors are being researched
  • Semiconductors are now a matter of national security
  • The US CHIPS Act (2022) invested $52B to onshore chip manufacturing
  • Taiwan produces ~90% of the world's most advanced chips — a geopolitical flashpoint
  • Whoever leads in transistor technology leads in AI, defense, and economic power

The transistor is the most transformative invention in human history.
Every digital device, every network, every AI — all begin with one tiny switch.
